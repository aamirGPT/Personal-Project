Education
After going through thick and thin, enjoying and sometimes getting bullied I passed my grade 10 with 64.40% from St. Xavier's High School affiliated with The Maharashtra State Board of Secondary & Higher Secondary Education in 2014. Well it was enough for me considering the amount of studies I did to back that up. I was more into 'go with the flow' kind of a guy.

I haven't been a very social person throughout my life and so I spent the next two years again goofing around with a friend whom I met in my Abhinav Junior College. We had three streams to choose from; Science, Commerce and Arts. I proceeded with commerce and took IT as my optional subject because I saw myself good with computers lol. Then my finals of Grade 10+2 arrived in 2016 and I passed out with decent 61.08%. I gotta admit I still miss those days.

With not much thinking I decided to go for Bachelors of Science in Information Technology as Software Engineers were very high in demand during those years, It was a three year course, which was meant to teach students fundamentals of programming and how to program programming concepts in languages such as C, C++, Java, Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, etc. and ~boy I was so confused at it. I somehow managed to clear the bachelors in 2019 with CGPA 6.22 out of 10.00 as I was not that great at programming and the teaching methods never really painted a picture of the topics inside my brain.

A year went by as I was searching for jobs as a software developer it was tough to land one with no real programming skills. And to my aid Covid 19 outbreak happened. It imposed a country wide lockdown for a couple of months which gave me quite some time to get my mind clear about my career, let's consider that as my break period. It was time to learn for real this time and get started with my career. After my friend and I from degree college discussed that it was time to make up for the time we wasted, we decided to enroll for a Masters Degree.

We chose Master's of Computer Application at Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies University (Mumbai). Now this was a two year course which was completed almost online. It is a rigourous course that made us create projects as final submissions for each subject. It really helped me grasp the basics well and I was making progress in learning as I implemented them in my projects. The languages I actually learned this time were C++, Java, Python, MySQL, Web Development Core Languages, Android Development, with projects developed in each language as final submissions. Unfortunately, I had no real time experience in using Github so the only place I know those projects are now available on is in college archives and my memories. ~Well 3 out of 4 semesters were just academics with projects submitted to our teachers, however the last semester was our internships that we landed with our on-campus interviews. I managed to land two internships / job offers through my masters with actual knowledge on how to write programs other than "Hello World!".
